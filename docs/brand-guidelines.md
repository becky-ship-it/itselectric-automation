# it's electric — Brand Guidelines

Source: itselectric.us (scraped April 2026)

---

## Mission

Democratize EV charging access in cities by installing curbside chargers at no cost to property owners or municipalities — enabling equitable clean transportation for urban residents who can't charge at home.

## Vision

A world where every city dweller has convenient charging access, regardless of income or housing type. **"The Future. It's Electric."**

---

## Brand Story

Founded by **Tiya Gordon** (design & operations) and **Nathan King** (sustainable architecture). Born from a real problem: owning an EV in NYC during the pandemic with nowhere to charge. Built a solution that works *behind the meter* — tapping underutilized electrical supply at partner properties to power curbside Level 2 chargers.

Backed by Uber, Brooklyn Bridge Ventures. Recognized by Fast Company, the World Economic Forum, and SXSW. Expanding from Brooklyn pilots into Detroit, Boston, and cities across the US.

## Tone of Voice

**Approachable, energetic, and forward-thinking.**

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Speak directly to real pain points | Use technical jargon |
| Lead with the problem, then the elegant solution | Lead with specs |
| Use confident, bold claims | Be mealy-mouthed or hedge |
| Celebrate community and mutual benefit | Sound corporate |
| Keep it human — real stories, real neighborhoods | Use generic stock phrases |

Voice is casual but credible. Playful enough to say "Power from the people!" but substantive enough to earn trust from city officials and investors in the same breath.

## Target Audiences

### Property Owners
**Motivator:** Passive income, zero hassle.
- Earn ~$1,000/charger/year
- No installation cost, no permit headaches — it's electric handles everything
- Frame as: a partnership, not a transaction

### EV Drivers & Aspiring EV Owners
**Motivator:** Charging access where they live.
- Removes the #1 barrier to EV ownership for urban renters
- Portable cable model means one cable works across the network
- Frame as: freedom, not infrastructure

### Cities & Municipalities
**Motivator:** Climate goals, no budget impact.
- Behind-the-meter approach costs cities nothing
- Scalable curbside solution compatible with existing permitting
- Frame as: a partner solving a hard problem, not a vendor selling hardware

### Developers & Institutions
**Motivator:** Sustainable urban infrastructure at scale.
- Partnership opportunities
- Frame as: future-proofing neighborhoods

## Visual Identity

### Logo
- Wordmark: **"it's electric"** — always lowercase
- Clean, modern sans-serif
- Consistent across all touchpoints

### Colors
- **Primary accent:** Orange / warm tones — energetic, optimistic
- **Secondary:** Rainbow gradient palette — used in headers and decorative elements; signals inclusivity and forward motion
- **Neutrals:** Black and white — clean, minimal foundation

### Typography
- Sans-serif dominant
- Modern and geometric
- Spacious layouts with generous whitespace
- No decorative or script fonts

### Imagery & Illustration Style
- Geometric SVG illustrations
- Minimalist, low-profile — mirrors the charger design philosophy
- Urban settings: sidewalks, curbs, city streets
- People-focused where photography is used

### Product Design Language
The charger itself is a brand statement:
- No screens
- No touchpads
- No fixed cables
- Low-profile, unobtrusive pole-mounted unit
- "Elegant and unobtrusive" — design should communicate the same

## What We Are Not

- Not a utility company
- Not a traditional EV hardware vendor
- Not for suburban homeowners with garages

## Writing Checklist

Before publishing any copy, verify:

- [ ] "it's electric" is lowercase (never "It's Electric" mid-sentence, never all-caps)
- [ ] Leads with the human problem, not the technology
- [ ] Avoids jargon ("Level 2" is fine internally; plain language externally)
- [ ] Speaks to at least one specific audience motivator
- [ ] Tone is confident, not boastful
- [ ] Reflects equity and access as a value, not a feature
